Internet Search

Whether it is Google, Yahoo, or Safari, many cannot imagine life today without access to search engines on the internet. Search gives us access to an infinite volume of information at our fingertips. This was not always the case. Before developing the technology, software engineers and programmers had to work tirelessly to convince investors that it will bring in sufficient revenue but it did not take very long before they proved their success.

Yahoo was created when two young electrical engineering students were looking for a way to win a Stanford University basketball league. They unknowingly took the first steps to “search”. Yahoo kicked off the web boom. Many startups began releasing new software and search engines, one of these being Excite which was founded by two tech-savvy Stanford students. The internet was booming and the competition was extremely high.

Excite wanted to partner with Google’s search technology to surpass yahoo who was dominating the search engine nation. Google was still very new at the time but had impressive technology that Excite wanted access to but after comparing the two searches side by side, Excite decided not to move forward because they did not see the potential of search. In hindsight they regretted the decision of passing on purchasing Google for only one million dollars at the time. Google then went on to become one of leading search engines in the world.

Revenue was a huge concern for Google’s investors because the site’s search page did not run ads but once they saw the potential and the range of audience the search engine was able to reach, investors quickly changed their minds. They then focused their work on mimicking the yellow pages in Search with tags and keywords. They attempted to collaboration with Overture but the two companies did not see eye-to-eye. Google then went to work on Adworks which was similar to the work Overture did. Overture then sued Google for copying their technology. Both companies eventually settled but it led to tension between them.

Google went public in 2004 and their stocks rapidly grew to surpass Yahoo, Overture and every other search engine of the time. Nonetheless, they continue to grow and expand their margins to stay at the top to surpass their top competitors such Microsoft.
